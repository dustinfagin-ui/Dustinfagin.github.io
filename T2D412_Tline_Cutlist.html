🧶 Damping Material Plan

Driver end → Start of Line ← Terminus / Open Mouth
Free Zone
First ~14" (Leg 1 top + Turn 1)
Damped — Long-Fiber Wool or Acousta-Stuf
Remaining ~78" of line
Why the free zone? Leaving the first section near the driver undamped lets the cone pressurize and load into the line properly. Damping too close to the driver reduces efficiency and output.

How much fill? Loosely pack (don't compress) long-fiber wool. A medium density fill — you should see through it, not compress it into a solid mass. Use staples or thin wire to hold material against walls rather than blocking the center path.

Recommended material: Acousta-Stuf polyfill (Best Buy / Parts Express), long-fiber sheep's wool, or open-cell acoustic foam strips along walls. Avoid closed-cell foam or fiberglass — wrong density.

28 Hz (ON)
Use the onboard 28 Hz subsonic switch. Protects driver below the 32 Hz tune point.
Bass Boost (PBC)
0 dB (OFF)
T-line provides natural acoustic gain. Boost risks over-excursion at 1,000W. Leave off.
Gain Setting
DMM Method
Set with 50Hz tone at −5 dBFS. Target: ~44.7V RMS across terminals (= 1,000W into 2Ω). Never set by ear at high volumes.
Remote Level
Start at 50%
Use remote level control (RFC) as a daily volume trim, not a gain substitute.

🔧 Recommended Build & Assembly Order

1

Cut All Panels

Use a circular saw with a straight-edge guide for all rips. Use a jigsaw with a circle jig for the 12" sub cutout in the baffle. Sand all cut edges with 80-grit.

2

Dry-Fit First

Assemble without glue or screws to verify all panels align, the line path flows correctly, and the sub sits flush. Mark all panel faces with a pencil before disassembly.

3

Install Divider Wall A (Fold Wall 1)

Glue and screw Divider A between the front and rear panels first, stopping 8.75" from the bottom. Use corner clamps and 1-5/8" drywall screws every 4–5". Pre-drill and countersink all MDF screws to avoid splitting.

4

Install Divider Wall B (Fold Wall 2)

Glue and screw Divider B starting from the top cap down, stopping 9.5" from the bottom. This creates your 3-leg fold path. Verify the interior width of each leg is 4.25" front-to-back at this stage.

5

Close the Box — Sides, Top, Bottom

Attach left side, right side, top cap, and bottom cap with PL Premium construction adhesive + screws. Seal all interior seams with silicone caulk or wood glue. Every seam must be airtight — use a flashlight inside and feel for airflow while a helper blows into the terminus.

6

Mount the Sub Baffle

Double up the baffle (glue two 3/4" layers together = 1.5" thick) for the T2D412's motor weight. Pre-drill speaker bolt holes (typically M6 or T-nuts) before gluing to the box. Use a foam gasket or weather stripping between sub and baffle for an airtight seal.

7

Install Damping Material

Starting 14" from the driver, loosely fill the rear 2/3 of the line with Acousta-Stuf or long-fiber wool. Staple to walls — do not pack tight. Leave the center of the line path clear. Do not damp Leg 3 near the terminus.

8

Wire, Test, and Tune

Wire both voice coils in parallel (2Ω). Run a 1/0 AWG power feed minimum to the T1000-1BDCP. Set gain with a DMM and 50 Hz test tone. Play music at moderate volume for 10 minutes and feel the cone — it should be warm but not hot, with no mechanical noise (slap, crunch, or bottoming).
